# Claude for Credit Unions: Practical Templates

## How to Use These

These are prompts you can paste directly into Claude. No coding experience needed. Copy, paste, and replace the parts in `[brackets]` with your own information.

Credit unions are financial co-ops — member-owned, community-rooted, and built on trust. These templates help with the operational work that eats up staff time so you can focus on members.

**Important:** Claude is a drafting and analysis tool, not a licensed financial advisor. All output should be reviewed by qualified staff before use with members. Never paste member PII (Social Security numbers, account numbers) into Claude.

## 1. Loan Document Summarizer

Paste this into Claude:

```
I'm a credit union loan officer. I need to explain this loan document
to a member in plain language. Our members are [describe typical member:
working families, farmers, small business owners, etc.].

Summarize this document covering:
1. What the member is agreeing to, in plain English
2. Monthly payment amount and total cost over the life of the loan
3. Any fees (origination, late payment, prepayment penalties)
4. What happens if they miss a payment
5. Their rights (rescission period, dispute process)
6. Anything unusual compared to a standard [loan type] agreement

Loan document:
[Paste loan agreement text here]

Write the summary at an 8th grade reading level. No legal jargon.
Format it as a one-page handout we can give the member to take home.
```

## 2. Member Communication Drafts

```
I work at [credit union name], a credit union serving [number] members
in [area/region].

Draft the following member communications:

1. RATE CHANGE NOTICE
   - [Savings/CD/loan] rates changing from [X]% to [Y]% effective [date]
   - Reason: [Federal Reserve action / market conditions / board decision]
   - Tone: Transparent, member-focused. Remind them they own this institution.

2. NEW SERVICE ANNOUNCEMENT
   - We're launching [service: mobile deposit / online loan apps /
     financial counseling / new branch / extended hours]
   - Available starting [date]
   - How to access it: [details]

3. ANNUAL MEETING INVITATION
   - Date: [date], Location: [venue]
   - Board election: [number] seats open, [candidates if known]
   - Topics: [year-in-review, dividend announcement, strategic plan]
   - Make it feel like a community event, not a corporate obligation

4. DELINQUENCY OUTREACH (compassionate)
   - For members who are [30/60/90] days past due on [loan type]
   - Offer options: payment plan, skip-a-pay, hardship modification,
     financial counseling referral
   - Tone: We're on your side. We want to help you keep this loan current.
   - This is NOT a collection threat. It's an outstretched hand.

Format each as ready-to-send. Include subject lines for emails.
```

## 3. Compliance Checklist Generator

```
Our credit union is in [state] with [asset size: under $50M / $50M-$250M /
$250M-$1B / over $1B].

We're regulated by [NCUA / state regulator name] and our last exam was [date].

Generate compliance checklists for:

1. BSA/AML (Bank Secrecy Act / Anti-Money Laundering)
   - CTR filing requirements and thresholds
   - SAR procedures and timeline
   - CDD (Customer Due Diligence) requirements
   - OFAC screening procedures
   - Training requirements for staff

2. CONSUMER LENDING COMPLIANCE
   - TILA disclosure requirements
   - ECOA/fair lending documentation
   - HMDA reporting (if applicable)
   - Flood insurance determination
   - MLA (Military Lending Act) requirements

3. QUARTERLY BOARD REPORTING
   - Financial ratios the board needs to see (net worth, delinquency,
     ROA, loan-to-share)
   - Comparison to peer credit unions
   - Regulatory requirement status
   - Policy review schedule

4. ANNUAL REQUIREMENTS CALENDAR
   - All regulatory filings with due dates
   - Required policy reviews
   - Staff training deadlines
   - Audit and exam prep timeline

Format as printable checklists with check boxes.
Our compliance officer is [one person / shared role / contracted].
Keep it practical — what do we actually need to DO, not just know about.
```

## 4. Financial Education Content

```
Our credit union wants to provide financial education to our members.

Member demographics:
- Community: [rural/urban/suburban], [region]
- Common occupations: [farming, manufacturing, service, etc.]
- Age range: [typical range]
- Common financial challenges: [list 2-3: first-time homebuyers,
  managing farm income variability, building credit, retirement
  planning on modest income, etc.]

Create the following educational materials:

1. ONE-PAGE GUIDES (printable, plain language):
   - "Understanding Your Credit Score" — what it is, how to check it
     free, 3 things that improve it, 3 things that hurt it
   - "First-Time Home Buyer Checklist" — steps from savings to closing,
     tailored to [our area's] housing market
   - "Building an Emergency Fund on Any Income" — realistic strategies,
     not "just save more"

2. WORKSHOP OUTLINE (1 hour):
   - Topic: [choose: budgeting basics / credit building / home buying /
     retirement planning / farm financial management / small business finances]
   - Hands-on activities, not lectures
   - Take-home worksheet
   - Follow-up resources at our credit union

3. YOUTH FINANCIAL LITERACY:
   - Program for ages [range] at [school/community center]
   - [4/6/8] session curriculum outline
   - Interactive activities appropriate for age group
   - Connection to credit union youth accounts

Tone: Respectful. Never condescending. These are smart people managing
complicated lives on tight margins. Meet them where they are.
```

## 5. Board Reporting & Strategic Planning

```
I'm the [CEO/manager] of [credit union name].

Our current numbers:
- Total assets: $[amount]
- Total members: [number]
- Net worth ratio: [X]%
- Loan-to-share ratio: [X]%
- Delinquency rate (60+ days): [X]%
- ROA: [X]%
- Number of staff: [number]
- Branches: [number and locations]

Year-over-year changes:
- Membership: [up/down X%]
- Loans: [up/down X%]
- Deposits: [up/down X%]
- Delinquency: [up/down X basis points]

Help me with:

1. MONTHLY BOARD PACKET
   - Executive summary (one page, narrative, not just numbers)
   - Key ratios with trend arrows and peer comparison context
   - Loan portfolio breakdown by type with concentrations flagged
   - Deposit trends and cost of funds
   - Membership growth/attrition analysis
   - Action items from previous meeting — status update

2. STRATEGIC PLAN DISCUSSION STARTER
   - Based on our numbers, what are our 3 biggest opportunities?
   - What are our 3 biggest risks?
   - Where are we strong compared to banks in our market?
   - Where are we vulnerable?
   - What should a 3-year strategic focus look like for a credit
     union our size in [our region]?

3. NCUA EXAM PREP
   - Based on our ratios, what will examiners likely focus on?
   - Draft responses to common examiner questions about [area of concern]
   - Organize our documentation checklist by CAMEL component

Format the board packet as a professional document. The strategic plan
section should be conversational — this is for board discussion, not a
consultant's report.
```

## How to Use Any of These

1. Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai)
2. Copy any template above
3. Replace the `[brackets]` with your real information
4. Press Enter
5. Review the output, ask follow-ups, refine

**Privacy reminder:** Never paste member PII (SSN, account numbers, DOB) into any AI tool. Use anonymized or sample data when working with templates that reference individual members.
